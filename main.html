How to Tune a Cello

A Basic Guide


Nessary "Equipment"
  • Have a Cello
  • Have a Cello Bow nearby if needed
    • If you will use the Cello bow you will need rosin for the bow
  • You will need a instrument tuner to make sure that the pitch of the cello is right
    • Some great tuners for your phone include insTuner.
    • One easy to use online tuner is tuner.ninja
    • Other great tuners include the Korg CA-2 Chromatic Tuner.

Now lets get into the basics.

Tuning a cello
    1. Make sure you have your tuner of choice next to you, turn it on.
    2. Pluck or bow the right-most string
    3. Check the tuner, the letter C should show up.
    4. If the letter C is left of the tuner, turn the tiny knobs at the bottom of the cello counterclockwise a couple turns
    5. Pluck or bow the right-most string (also the thickest string)
    6. Check the tuner, the letter C should show up.
    7. If the letter C is right of the tuner, turn the tiny knobs at the bottom of the cello clockwise a couple turns
    8. Repeat this process until the letter C is in the middle of the screen
    To tune the other strings, repeat the same process but with the G-string (2nd thickest string) and its corresponding knob, the D-string (the second-thinnest string) and its corresponding knob, and with the A-string (the thinnest string) and its corresponding knob.
